The Problem

Context

Throughout Utah, high school lunches often lack sufficient nutrition. Despite existing programs, many students remain hungry or miss essential nutrients.

Contributing Factors

1. Small portion sizes and limited menu variety.

2. Budget constraints push schools towards cheaper, processed foods.

Consequences

Poor nutrition can lead to low energy, decreased focus in class, and increased risk of obesity and chronic diseases over time.

The Solution

Organizations Involved

BMC Public Health: Implements nutrition education and supportive environments in schools (Teo et al.).

Journal of School Health: Enforces policies to limit junk food and increase healthy options (Merlo et al.).

How Can We Help?

- Start or join a student wellness/nutrition club.

- Volunteer at local food banks or school garden projects.

- Advocate for policy changes in school board meetings.
